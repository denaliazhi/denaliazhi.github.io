Tableau

Personal project

Type

Web scraping, data viz

Tools

Python, Tableau

Timespan

1 week, July 2021

Over this summer, I wanted to improve my web scraping and data visualization skills, so I decided to create a mini-series that explored the impact of Covid-19 on three spheres: vacancies, travel, and unemployment.

Process

NYC Rental vacancies

At the time, I had just started my first apartment hunt, so I was curious to know how the city's rental inventory had changed as a result of the pandemic. I found that StreetEasy offered a handy data dashboard with the information that I needed.

After downloading the csv file, I created my first viz in Tableau.


I used an area chart to compare the monthly rental inventory of 2019 to 2020. Evidently, the total number of vacancies was much greater during the latter half of 2020 than in 2019.

Through this viz, I learned how to pivot columns, apply data filters, synchronize dual axes, and format tooltips.

U.S. air travel

Having been at home for most of the last year and a half, I wondered about the extent to which air travel was impacted by the pandemic. I found that the Transportation Security Administration offered a table with daily traveler throughput on their website, which I scraped using a simple Python script.


The initial line chart derived from the raw data was quite jagged, so I decided to calculate a moving average. This lighter blue line shows a general trend without day-to-day fluctuations.

As seen above, traveler throughput dropped sharply in early 2020 and remained low throughout much of that year. However, by August 2021, it had almost returned to pre-pandemic levels.

Industry unemployment

Finally, I wanted to identify the industries that had been most and least affected by the pandemic. The Bureau of Economic Analysis provided data on each industry's workforce by year.


For this viz, I created a calculated field to get the percent change in the workforce between 2019 and 2020 for each industry. I wrote a conditional statement to color the bars based on the direction of the change. I also learned how to add a viz-in-tooltip.

Based on the results, the arts, food services, and mining industries suffered the biggests employment losses in 2020, whereas the transportation and finance industries actually gained employees.
