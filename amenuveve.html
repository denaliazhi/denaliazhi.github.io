Amenuveve

Website redesign

Type

UI / UX

Tools

Wix editor, JS

Timespan

May 2020 - Present

Since 2020, I've been the designer and webmaster for Amenuveve, a nonprofit in Ghana. This organization is based on an ongoing partnership between the village of Woadze Tsatoe and a social impact course at New York University.

I have complete two redesigns for Amenuveve’s website, both of which have accompanied major shifts in its mission and status.

Process

First Redesign

Amenuveve was initially a single business that was founded to empower the village women through income from batik, or the art of block-printing. A basic online store had been set up by previous students to allow visitors to view and order the batik products.

As new enterprises formed over the years, the community desired a website that could reflect the broadened scope of their work.


Throughout July 2020, I met virtually with a team of volunteers and villagers to discuss features of the redesign. My goal was to create a modern and dynamic site that would serve three purposes:

  1. Document the progress of major projects

  2. Showcase everyday life in the village

  3. Streamline ordering and donations processes

By the end of August, I had completed and published my very first redesign using Wix. We had chosen a no-code platform builder in order to make management of the site accessible to all team members. The now archived site can be viewed here.

Second Redesign


Later on, the team chose to nest all current and future programs under the Amenuveve name and register it as a nonprofit. As a result, the website required a more formal appearance and better documentation to build the organization's credibility.

I approached this second redesign in a more structured manner. First, I worked on outlining the information architecture for the website. Then, I created mockups on Figma, meeting weekly with the team to present each new addition and to revise the content or layout of the pages.


Finally, I transformed the mockups into a functioning website through Wix. I even wrote some custom Javascript to implement features that weren't readily available, such as an image slider to display volunteer roles on the "Get involved" page.

This second redesign was published in August 2021 and can be viewed here.
