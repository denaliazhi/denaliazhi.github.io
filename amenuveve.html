Amenuveve

Website redesign

Type

UI / UX

Tools

Figma, Wix

Timespan

May 2020 - Sep 2021

For over a year, I was the creative lead at Amenuveve, a nonprofit born from collaboration between a village in Ghana and a social impact course at New York University.

One of my contributions was the two-part redesign of its website, a process that would reflect the growth of both the organization and the designer (me 🙂).

Process

First Redesign

In 2015, students of New York University traveled to Woadze Tsatoe to lay the groundwork of a partnership, literally, through the construction of a school, a water well, and a public restroom. At that time, the village had sustained itself wholly through fishing and farming.

Over the next five years, students would help to build enterprises that could steer the community beyond these subsistence activities. The first of such enterprises was a batik center. Through the traditional art of batik, or block-printing, residents learned to craft beautiful, vibrant products that could be sold at a premium in international markets. A basic website was set up to showcase these products.

In 2020, I enrolled in the affiliated course at New York University. By this time, additional projects were underway such as a scholarship fund, a women's health program, and a clean water business. The website, however, remained a static catalogue for batik products.


That semester, I proposed a more interactive, story-based site that could☝🏻(1) capture the progress of key initatives and ✌🏻(2) paint a fuller picture of life in the village. With a Social Impact Stipend from the university, I spent the following summer on my idea.

This redesign was my first major project involving stakeholders beyond, well, myself. Each week, I would build one or two pages of the site and present the results to a team of volunteers and village members for their feedback. The creative direction, however, was left largely in my hands; in this way, I was incredibly lucky to have the level of trust and autonomy that was granted to me by the team early on.

After a few months of design and review, the website was published.

Second Redesign

In 2021, all community projects were brought under a 401(c) nonprofit called Amenuveve with an advisory board spread across the U.S., Canada, and Ghana. The organization sought to rebrand the website under this name and requested a second redesign.

By now, I had a few design courses and certifications under my belt, which informed my approach to this process. Before a single page was rendered, I was already meeting with the advisory board to hone its brand identity and with users to uncover existing pain points.

The nonprofit's main interest was in fundraising for its various programs, so the website would need clear calls to action and a streamlined donation form. Users wanted transparency in their donations, so the website would need data-driven impact statements. Based on ideas like these, I mapped out the structure of the site.

Once the information architecture was in place, I created wireframes in Figma. From wireframes, I moved on to mockups. From mockups, I developed a working prototype. Involving the team in each stage of this process led to more efficient and informed decisions about the design.
